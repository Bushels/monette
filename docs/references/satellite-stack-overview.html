Sentinel-1 — VH polarization (PRIMARY)

COPERNICUS/S1_GRD_FLOAT · IW · descending

High
Spectrum / Measurement C-band SAR (5.6 cm wavelength), cross-polarization (vertical transmit, horizontal receive). Volume-scattering sensitivity to vegetation and standing residue.
What it tells us Primary seeding signal. We compute ΔVH = 10·log₁₀(T1median / T0median) per parcel. +1.5 dB → seeded; -1.5 dB → not seeded; in-between → null. Validated in May 1 PA + Raymore smokes.
GEE refresh ~6 days @ SK latitude (S1A + S1C constellation, descending pass)
Caveats: 14-day rolling T1 median; orbit-coverage gaps for territories spanning >3° latitude (orbit pinning dropped in v1).

Sentinel-1 — VV polarization (mask)

COPERNICUS/S1_GRD_FLOAT · IW · descending

High
Spectrum / Measurement C-band SAR co-polarization (vertical transmit, vertical receive). Surface-roughness sensitivity.
What it tells us Used in the common valid-pixel mask alongside VH (per Codex Bucket A round 2 fix). NOT used as a primary seeding signal in v1. v1.1 backlog: derive VH/VV ratio as no-till-vs-tillage discriminator.
GEE refresh ~6 days (paired acquisition with VH)

Sentinel-2 — SCL (snow flag)

COPERNICUS/S2_SR_HARMONIZED · band SCL · class 11

High
Spectrum / Measurement Scene Classification Layer at 20m. Class 11 = snow (per ESA SCL definition).
What it tells us Per-pixel snow detection within parcel AOI. If snow_pct ≥ 5% at the latest SAR scene date → veto with reason snow_or_freeze_risk. Validated in today's smoke (Raymore SW-7 cleared, others vetoed).
GEE refresh ~3-5 days @ SK latitude (5-day equator nominal, faster polewards)
Caveats: Small bboxes can hit zero S2 scenes in cloudy windows → ERA5 fallback fires. ±3-day lookup window per scene.

Sentinel-2 — NDVI

COPERNICUS/S2_SR_HARMONIZED · (B8 - B4) / (B8 + B4)

High
Spectrum / Measurement Normalized Difference Vegetation Index. Near-infrared (B8, 842 nm) vs Red (B4, 665 nm). Single-best scene picker, NOT median composite (Codex Bucket B design call).
What it tells us Vegetation greenness. Parcel-mean NDVI > 0.25 gates the tillage indices off (field is too vegetated for residue indices to be physically meaningful).
GEE refresh ~3-5 days (same as SCL)

Sentinel-2 — NDTI (Tillage Index)

COPERNICUS/S2_SR_HARMONIZED · (B11 - B12) / (B11 + B12)

Med-High
Spectrum / Measurement Normalized Difference Tillage Index. SWIR1 (B11, 1610 nm) vs SWIR2 (B12, 2190 nm). Exploits cellulose/lignin absorption features in 2100-2300 nm range.
What it tells us High NDTI = residue cover preserved (consistent with no-till seeding). Low NDTI = bare soil exposed (consistent with weed-control tillage). Used in optical-features schema for operator review; NOT yet in the decision rule.
GEE refresh ~3-5 days
Caveats: Crop-and-season specific. Soil moisture variation can confound. Doc recommends complementing with narrow-band SINDRI (deferred).

Sentinel-2 — BSI (Bare Soil Index)

COPERNICUS/S2_SR_HARMONIZED · ((B11+B4)-(B8+B2)) / ((B11+B4)+(B8+B2))

Med
Spectrum / Measurement Bare Soil Index combining SWIR1, Red, NIR, Blue. Higher = more bare soil exposed.
What it tells us Auxiliary signal for cropland verification. Less commonly used than NDVI/NDTI; included in optical schema for completeness.
GEE refresh ~3-5 days

ERA5-Land — Air temperature

ECMWF/ERA5_LAND/DAILY_AGGR · temperature_2m

High
Spectrum / Measurement Daily mean 2m air temperature in Kelvin. ECMWF reanalysis at ~9 km grid.
What it tells us Temperature must exceed 273.15 K (0°C) at the SAR scene date for ground to be unfrozen. Below threshold → veto with snow_or_freeze_risk. v1.1 backlog: also accumulate Growing Degree Days as agronomic gate.
GEE refresh Daily, ~5-9 day publication lag
Caveats: Late-window scenes silently lose ERA5 contribution if scene date falls within publication lag. Pipeline guards via era5_size().gt(0) check.

ERA5-Land — 24h precipitation

ECMWF/ERA5_LAND/DAILY_AGGR · total_precipitation_sum

Med
Spectrum / Measurement Daily total precipitation in metres (we ×1000 → mm). ECMWF reanalysis at ~9 km grid.
What it tells us Max precipitation across all S1 contributing scene dates > 5 mm/24h → wet-soil veto (false-positive risk in SAR backscatter). Per Codex Bucket B design call: max-across-S1-contributing, NOT mean-across-window.
GEE refresh Daily, ~5-9 day publication lag
Caveats: 9 km grid smooths over local convective storms. Negative artifact clamped to 0.

ERA5-Land — Snow cover (fallback)

ECMWF/ERA5_LAND/DAILY_AGGR · snow_cover

Med
Spectrum / Measurement Snow cover fraction (0-1) at ~9 km grid. Used as fallback when Sentinel-2 SCL has no scenes in the ±3-day window.
What it tells us Coarse snow signal. Single 5% threshold applies to both S2 SCL and ERA5 fraction.
GEE refresh Daily, ~5-9 day publication lag
Caveats: 9 km grid is too coarse for parcel-scale truth. Used only when S2 unavailable. In winter prairies it stays at ~68% even where roads/south-facing slopes are bare.

USDA CDL 2025 — US cropland

USDA/NASS/CDL/2025 · 30m raster

High
Spectrum / Measurement Per-pixel crop-class classification at 30m, USDA NASS official annual product. Classes mapped to applicability via applicability.py.
What it tells us Cropland coverage % per parcel; per-pixel crop class drives applicability (perennial → no seeding event detectable; winter wheat → out-of-season in spring).
GEE refresh Annual, ~6-8 month lag
Caveats: CDL 2025 only fully published in spring 2026. For early-season runs we may fall back to CDL 2024.

AAFC ACI 2024 — Canadian cropland

AAFC/ACI/2024 · 30m raster

High
Spectrum / Measurement Annual Crop Inventory at 30m. Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada official product covering Canadian cropland.
What it tells us Same role as CDL but for SK/MB/AB properties. Cropland coverage + applicability per parcel.
GEE refresh Annual, similar lag to CDL
Caveats: AAFC ACI 2025 not yet in GEE catalog as of run date; 2024 used conservatively.

Under evaluation (v1.5 scope) — 3 candidates

Sentinel-1 SLC → InSAR Coherence

External via SNAP / openEO · NOT GEE-native

v1.5
Spectrum / Measurement Phase coherence between consecutive SAR acquisitions. Detects millimeter-scale physical disturbance — exactly the spatial scale of a no-till drill press wheel.
What it would tell us Direct detection of the physical seeding event independent of bulk surface roughness. Chen et al. 2025 IEEE: RMSE 5.59 days (dual-orbit Ontario corn). Codex 2026-05-01 review: Option D — confidence booster only, not primary signal in v1.
GEE refresh N/A — GEE doesn't ingest S1 SLC Phase information lost in pyramiding.
Status: Only path is external SNAP/openEO processing (e.g., Copernicus openEO sentinel1_sar_coherence launched April 2026), with parcel statistics ingested back. Effort: 2-4 day spike + 1-2 weeks rollout after local labels accrue. Full Codex review.

RADARSAT Constellation Mission (RCM)

CSA · 3-satellite C-band SAR with compact polarimetry

v1.5
Spectrum / Measurement C-band SAR with circular-polarization transmit, dual-linear receive. Enables m-chi decomposition into volume / surface / double-bounce scattering components.
What it would tell us V/S scattering ratio differentiates no-till seeding (stable V/S) from weed-control tillage (V/S drops). Canadian-specific advantage: 4-day revisit vs S1's 6-day.
GEE refresh N/A — not in GEE catalog Requires CSA data access agreement.
Status: Requires data ingestion infrastructure + non-trivial m-chi decomposition. Effort: 1-2 weeks. Strong Canadian-specific value but defer until CCD shadow-spike clarifies whether RCM is needed.

Copernicus DEM → Topographic Wetness Index

COPERNICUS/DEM/GLO30 · 30m DEM

v1.5
Spectrum / Measurement Global digital elevation model at 30m. TWI = ln(catchment_area / tan(slope)) — identifies depressional zones where water accumulates.
What it would tell us Static slough mask for Prairie Pothole Region properties. Combined with dynamic SAR-based slough detection (specular open water + double-bounce flooded vegetation), generates per-parcel arable-zone mask that excludes slough geometry.
GEE refresh DEM static; SAR slough detection 6 days
Status: Most relevant for Eddystone, Kamsack, Manitoba parkland properties. Not blocking current SK properties. Effort: 2-3 days. Defer until northern SK/MB baseline-blocked properties unblock (mid-May).

Rejected — 4 reasons

PlanetScope (Planet Labs)

Commercial · ~3m daily Blue/Green/Red/NIR

Rejected
Why rejected Commercial pricing material for ~250k acres across 14 properties. Free Sentinel-1 + Sentinel-2 sufficient for "is parcel seeded yes/no" use case at the cadence we need. PlanetScope solves a problem (in-season management decisions at 3m daily) we don't have.

U-Net / SAM2 boundary delineation

Learned segmentation on optical imagery

Rejected
Why rejected We have authoritative cadastral data (DLS quarter sections in Canada, PLSS sections in the US). Title boundaries are legally definitive — learned boundaries from imagery would be less accurate, not more. Useful for crowd-sourced or unknown-owner mapping; not relevant for CCAA-creditor tracking.

Random Forest / XGBoost as primary classifier

ML decision rule over multi-sensor features

Rejected v1
Why rejected (for v1) No ground-truth seeded labels yet. 533 active parcels too small for robust RF/XGBoost training without overfitting (cited studies used thousands of labeled fields). Rule-based decision logic is interpretable and auditable — important for CCAA monitoring. Reconsider for v1.5+ once vote-label loop accrues 50-100+ labels.

75-85% zonal threshold AS PRIMARY rule

% of pixels above ΔVH threshold replaces parcel mean

Rejected as primary
Why rejected as primary Could mask legitimate partial-seeding situations. If a farmer hasn't seeded headlands yet, we want to surface that as partial_signal not silently classify the whole parcel. Adopted as a SECONDARY signal (see v1.1 backlog) where per-pixel distribution complements the mean reduction.
